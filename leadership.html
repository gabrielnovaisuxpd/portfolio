How I lead

Four things I don't compromise on.

01

Coaching over doing

I invest in growing designers through 1:1s, direct feedback, and stretch assignments — not by taking over their problems. At TradersClub this meant developing 4 coordinators who could run their own teams; at TownSq it means individual development plans tied to real project ownership, not just performance reviews.

02

Process as leverage

Good rituals — Design Review, Continuous Discovery, structured critique — are the infrastructure that lets teams operate at high quality without heroics. I built TownSq's Design Review system from scratch and it's now the reason design quality scores go up even as the team scales.

03

Radical candor

Direct feedback, delivered with care. I have the hard conversations early so teams don't waste months on misaligned assumptions. Designers who've worked with me consistently point to this as the thing that accelerated their careers the most — even when it was uncomfortable in the moment.

04

Cross-functional trust

Design earns influence through partnership, not through gatekeeping. I align closely with Product, Engineering, and Operations — and with executive stakeholders — so design decisions show up in the metrics leadership already cares about: NPS, retention, support load.
